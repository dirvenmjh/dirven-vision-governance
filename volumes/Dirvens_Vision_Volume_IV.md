Marinus's work has always been international in scope. Not because he sought prestige, but because the problems he tackles **do not respect borders**.

- The Netherlands

- The United Arab Emirates
- Belgium
- Pan-European consortia

**1. Systems behave differently across cultures**

Despite cultural differences, the core principles of:

- Transparency

- Ethical governance

…are universal human needs.

This global perspective is one of the reasons his frameworks scale.

Marinus's worldview is built on a principle that is both simple and profound:

He believes that a society should:

- Detect harm early
- Understand behavior contextually
- Align incentives toward cooperation
- Protect cognitive integrity

- Make ethical behavior the path of least resistance
